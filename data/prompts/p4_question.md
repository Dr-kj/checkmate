$$1^3 = 1$$

$$2^3 = 3 + 5$$

$$3^3 = 7 + 9 + 11$$

$$4^3 = 13 + 15 + 17 + 19 $$

$$\vdots$$


In general:

$\forall n \in \mathbb{N}_{>0}: n^3 = \left( {n^2 - n + 1} \right) + \left( {n^2 - n + 3} \right) + \cdots + \left( {n^2 + n - 1} \right)$

In particular, the first term for $\left( {n + 1} \right)^3$ is $2$ greater than the last term for $n^3$.
